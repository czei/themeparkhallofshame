Why This Site Exists

Let's get this out of the way right up front: the name is pure hyperbole. The fact is no one is going to look at a website called "theme park uptime percentages"; that would be too boring. I created the site because all of the complaining that theme parks "aren't what it used to be", all because a ride had the temerity to go down during someone's vacation. I'm an engineer, so hard numbers are the most valuable and useful things in the world. If we're going to have a discussion about theme park reliability, let's start with real numbers, not someone's once a year vacation anecdote.

What you may not realize is there's nothing harder than keeping an attraction working through a million visitors a year. Designing and maintaining something that has to work millions of time a year with zero issues is orders of magnitude more difficult than any ordinary engineering challenge. This is absolutely NOT an indictment of the men and women who maintain our theme parks.

But when I tried and find comparisons between theme parks, none existed. There were plenty of sites that would tell you the wait times for a particular ride, but nothing comparing parks and rides to each other.

The other big contrevoursy is which parks have the longest wait times. "No one goes there anymore, its too crowded". Again, there aren't any hard numbers online to easily parks to each other, so that's the second big analysis on this website.

How Scoring Works

The problem with this little project is fairly judging one park against another is way more difficult than you'd think. If you just add up the ride downtime, that penalizes parks with more rides. If you just go with uptime percentage, you're penalizing parks that have a bunch of complicated rides instead of simple old-fashioned carnival-style rides.

The Shame Score

The Shame Score is a normalized metric that measures how much a theme park's downtime hurts guests, accounting for both the amount of downtime and the importance of the rides that went down.

Shame Score = Total Weighted Downtime Hours ÷ Total Park Weight

Where:

  • Weighted Downtime = Each ride's downtime hours × its tier weight
  • Total Park Weight = Sum of all ride tier weights in the park

Ride Tiers

Every ride is classified into one of three tiers based on guest demand and impact:

Tier Weight Description Examples
1 3× Flagship attractions guests plan trips around Space Mountain, Hagrid's, Velocicoaster
2 2× Major rides with consistent demand Pirates of the Caribbean, Haunted Mansion
3 1× Standard attractions, lower guest impact Carousels, smaller flat rides

Why This Works

Problem: Raw downtime hours unfairly penalize larger parks. A park with 50 rides will naturally have more total downtime than a park with 20 rides. And 1 hour of downtime on Space Mountain hurts more guests than 1 hour on a carousel.

Solution: The Shame Score solves both problems:

  1. Normalizes for park size — Dividing by total park weight means a small park and large park with similar reliability get similar scores
  2. Weights by guest impact — Flagship ride downtime counts 3× more than minor ride downtime, reflecting actual guest disappointment

Example

Park A (small park): 1 Tier 1 ride down for 2 hours = 6 weighted hours. Total park weight = 15. Shame Score = 0.40

Park B (large park): 3 Tier 3 rides down for 2 hours each = 6 weighted hours. Total park weight = 45. Shame Score = 0.13

Park A has a higher shame score because losing a flagship ride hurts more than losing three minor rides, even though total downtime hours were the same.

Interpreting Scores

Score Range Interpretation
0.00 – 0.10Excellent reliability
0.10 – 0.30Good performance
0.30 – 0.50Average
0.50 – 1.00Below average
> 1.00Poor reliability

Higher score = More shame = Worse performance

Our Data Source

All live wait time and ride status data is powered by ThemeParks.wiki, an open-source API that aggregates real-time data from 50+ theme parks worldwide.

ThemeParks.wiki provides live data including:

  • Wait times - Current queue lengths for attractions
  • Operating status - Whether rides are Operating, Down (breakdown), Closed (scheduled), or in Refurbishment
  • Park schedules - Opening and closing times

The project is open-source under the Apache-2.0 license. You can view the source code on GitHub.

We collect snapshots every 5 minutes when parks are open, then aggregate this data to calculate downtime statistics and wait time trends over time.
