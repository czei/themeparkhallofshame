Why This Site Exists

Let's get this out of the way right up front: the name is pure hyperbole. Let's face it: no one is going to look at a website called "theme park uptime percentages". I created the site because all of the online shit-talking about which theme park company "isn't what it used to be", all because a ride had the temerity to go down during someone's vacation. I'm an engineer, so hard numbers are the most valuable and useful things in the world. If we're going to have a discussion about theme park reliability, let's start with real numbers, not someone's once a year vacation recolections.

What you may not realize is there's nothing harder than keeping an attraction working through a million visitors a year. Designing and maintaining something that has to work millions of time a year with zero issues is orders of magnitude more difficult than any ordinary engineering challenge. This is absolutely NOT an indictment of the men and women who maintain our theme parks.

But when I tried and find comparisons between theme parks, none existed. There were plenty of sites that would tell you the wait times for a particular ride, but nothing comparing parks and rides to each other.

The other big contrevoursy is which parks have the longest wait times. "No one goes there anymore, its too crowded". Again, there aren't any hard numbers online comparing each park, so that's the second big analysis on this website.
